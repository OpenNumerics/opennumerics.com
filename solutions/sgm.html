Reliable Bayesian Parameter Estimation for Real Engineering Systems

Many engineering workflows require rapid generation of physically realistic solution fields, not just a single high-accuracy simulation. In porous-media transport, this need arises in uncertainty quantification, design exploration, and real-time decision-making — settings where thousands of solution realizations may be required, but full PDE simulations are too slow to be used interactively.

In our client's battery project, transport occurs through a heterogeneous porous medium whose microscopic structure is not fully known. Uncertainty is modeled through a spatially varying porosity field $\varepsilon(x)$, which directly affects diffusivity and conductivity. The first figure shows a model of this system. For a fixed set of macroscopic operating conditions, different realizations of $\varepsilon(x)$ lead to distinct solution fields for concentration $c(x)$ and electric potential $\varphi(x)$. Accurately capturing this variability is essential for realistic modeling, but solving the PDE with each $\varepsilon(x)$ explicitly is computationally expensive.

Beyond micro-scale uncertainty, the system behavior is strongly influenced by a small number of macroscopic conditioning parameters. In this project, we condition on:

  • Typical spatial scale $l$ of $\varepsilon(x)$
  • Voltage $U_0$ applied over the battery
  • Boundary current density $I_{\text{right}}$

Together, these parameters define the operating regime of the system and determine how transport, reaction, and electric effects interact. For each choice of $(l, U_0, I_{\text{right}})$, there exists not a single solution, but a distribution of admissible solutions arising from unresolved micro-scale structure.

To efficiently model this conditional distribution, we turn to score-based generative models (SGMs) — a class of probabilistic models originally developed for high-quality image generation. Rather than predicting one deterministic solution, SGMs learn how to sample full solution fields from the conditional distribution $$p\big(c(x), \varphi(x) \mid l, U_0, I_{\text{right}}\big),$$ to capture both physical trends and inherent variability.

The model is trained on high-fidelity finite-volume simulations, where each training example consists of a solution pair $(c(x), \varphi(x))$ together with its associated conditioning parameters. Spatial structure is handled by convolutional networks, while global parameters and time-like diffusion variables are injected through feature-wise modulation (FiLM). The neural network architecture is displayed here: During inference, the trained SGM generates new solution realizations via a fast stochastic sampling process.

Once trained, the model produces diverse, physically plausible solution fields orders of magnitude faster than direct PDE simulation.
